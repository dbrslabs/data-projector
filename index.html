GUARDIAN GALAXY

A joint project from
The Guardian and DBRS Innovation Labs

GUARDIAN GALAXY is a visualization of articles from The Guardian using machine learning tools k-means and t-SNE. Each cluster is a selection of articles grouped together by machine learning algorithms which use multiple ways to look for sematic similarties. The 3D visualization is a representation of a more complex multidimensional clustering model. Articles in the Galaxy are from the Science section of the Guardian from 1921-2016.

Learn more about GUARDIAN GALAXY at website link.

Each circle below represents a group of related articles called a cluster. A cluster is a selection of articles grouped together by machine learning algorithms. The words of each article are turned into vector-based representations, similar articles are clustered together based on similar vector representations, and then the vector size is reduced to have 3 coordinates so similar articles can be plotted close to each other as clusters in 3D.

To see all or none of the clusters, click on the grey button or left-most button.

The article list displays one section at a time. To update or refresh the article list, click on Show Articles. Each time you click Show Articles, a new list of articles from the selected clusters is generated. The circles beside each headline represent the cluster to which each article belongs.
